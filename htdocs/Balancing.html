DEFINITION OF CHEMICAL TERMS

  • Chemical Change can be observed when there is a change in color, a production of gas, or a formation of precipitate.
  • A Chemical Reaction is taking place when a substance undergoes a chemical change.
  • Chemical Equations are reactions which reactants and products are expressed as formulas.
  • Diatomic Molecules are pure elements that form molecules consisting of two atoms bonded together.

PARTS OF A CHEMICAL EQUATION

STEPS IN BALANCING CHEMICAL EQUATIONS

  1. Identify the most complex substance. Usually, Oxygen and Hydrogen are balanced last. Do Accounting.
  2. Adjust the coefficients.
  3. Balance polyatomic ions as a unit.
  4. Balance the remaining atoms.
  5. Check your work. The number of atoms of each element in the reactants' part should be the same with that in the products' part.

FEW THINGS TO REMEMBER WHEN BALANCING CHEMICAL EQUATIONS

  1. Atoms are conserved. In a chemical reaction, they are neither lost nor gained and do not change their identity. Therefore, the same number of atoms of each kind must appear in the products.

  2. Subscripts cannot be altered in the correct formula of a compound. Changing the number or placement of the subscript means changing the identity of a compound as well as the meaning of the entire equation.

  3. Coefficients used (placed in front) multiplies everything in the symbols or formulas by that number.
