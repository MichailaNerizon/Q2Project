Cations Anions Ionic Compounds



Watch this video by Melissa Maribel:

  1. Cations formed from metal atoms have the same name as the metal.
  2. If a metal can form cations with different charges, the positive charge is indicated by a Roman numeral in parentheses following the name of the metal.
  3. If a metal can form cations with different charges, classical method can be used.
  1. The names of monatomic anions are formed by replacing the ending of the name of the element with -ide.
  2. In naming oxyanions, the -ate is used for the most common or representative oxyanion of an element, and -ite is used for an oxyanion that has the same charge but one Oxygen atom fewer.
  3. Prefixes are used when the series of oxyanions of an element extends to four members, as with the halogens.
  • >> These are generally composed of cations bonded to anions, as in NaCl and always represented as empirical formulas.

  • >> METALS tend to form CATION, and NON-METALS tend to form ANION.
  1. Write the cation name (space) followed by the anion name.

WRITING FORMULA OF IONIC COMPOUNDS

The ions in an ionic compound always occur in such a ratio that the total positive charge equals the total negative charge. In short, we can use CRISS-CROSS method to write the formula.




  1. Determine cation and charge.
  2. Determine anion and charge.
  3. Do crisscross method.
  4. Simplify if necessary.
